ABOUT THE NEIGHBORHOOD

THOUGHTFULLY PLANNED. BEAUTIFULLY LIVED.

Pacific Highlands Ranch is one of San Diego's most thoughtfully designed master-planned communities — built with a clear vision for modern family living at its finest. Located along the SR-56 corridor in the 92130 zip code, it sits adjacent to Carmel Valley and shares many of the same sought-after attributes: award-winning schools, proximity to the coast, and access to world-class retail and dining.

The community was carefully developed in phases, with The Village at Pacific Highlands Ranch serving as its vibrant town center — offering an upscale retail and dining destination anchored by familiar favorites and unique local gems alike. The community's layout prioritizes walkability, green space, and connectivity, with parks, trails, and open space woven throughout the neighborhoods.

Homes in Pacific Highlands Ranch tend toward newer construction — generally built between the 2010s and present — offering modern floor plans, energy-efficient features, and the architectural variety that comes with a thoughtfully master-planned development. From attached townhomes to expansive detached single-family residences, there is a home type and price point for a wide range of buyers.

For families relocating to San Diego — particularly those seeking excellent public schools and a true neighborhood feel — Pacific Highlands Ranch is consistently one of the first communities we recommend. Demand here is strong and inventory is typically limited, which speaks to how much buyers love it once they arrive.

COMMON QUESTIONS

COMMON QUESTIONS ABOUT PACIFIC HIGHLANDS RANCH

What zip code is Pacific Highlands Ranch?

Pacific Highlands Ranch is located in the 92130 zip code, shared with neighboring Carmel Valley. It sits along the SR-56 corridor in northern San Diego — close to the coast and well-positioned for commuting throughout the county.

When was Pacific Highlands Ranch built?

Development began in the early 2000s and continued through the 2010s and beyond. PHR was built in phases, so homes range from early 2000s construction to some of the most recently completed neighborhoods in San Diego. Many of the most sought-after communities were built between 2005 and 2015.

How many homes are in Pacific Highlands Ranch?

Pacific Highlands Ranch is made up of 38 distinct neighborhoods, collectively comprising several thousand homes — from attached townhomes and condominiums to large detached single-family residences. The variety of product types makes it one of the most accessible master-planned communities in San Diego.

What is the HOA fee in Pacific Highlands Ranch?

HOA fees vary by neighborhood — each of the 38 communities has its own HOA structure, with some also participating in a master community association. Fees typically range from under $100 to several hundred dollars per month depending on the community and its amenities. We’re happy to pull specific HOA details for any neighborhood you’re considering.

Is Pacific Highlands Ranch a good place to raise a family?

Consistently, yes — it’s one of the first communities we recommend for relocating families. The combination of top-performing schools, walkable neighborhood design, abundant parks and trails, and a genuine community atmosphere makes PHR particularly well-suited for families with children of all ages.

How far is Pacific Highlands Ranch from the beach?

About 10 to 12 minutes from Del Mar’s beaches — close enough to enjoy the coast regularly, yet far enough inland to offer newer construction, more generous lot sizes, and more competitive price points than the immediate coastal communities.
